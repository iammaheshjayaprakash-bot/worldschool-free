Ages 3–6

Explorers

The wonder years. Children are natural scientists — they touch, taste, ask, and imagine. This stage channels that energy into structured discovery across 6 core areas through play, story, and hands-on creation.

Stage Overview — Explorers (Ages 3–6)

Emphasis on play-based discovery, language acquisition, number sense, and social-emotional security. All 6 subjects are delivered through stories, songs, movement, and hands-on activities.

📚

Language & Literacy

  • Phonemic awareness — sounds, rhymes, syllables
  • Letter recognition (upper & lowercase)
  • Sight words — first 100 high-frequency words
  • Listening comprehension & story recall
  • Oral storytelling & vocabulary building
  • Pre-writing skills — pencil grip, tracing, patterns
  • Reading simple sentences
🔢

Number & Early Maths

  • Counting 1–20 with one-to-one correspondence
  • Number recognition and writing 0–10
  • Shapes — 2D and basic 3D forms
  • Patterns — identifying and extending sequences
  • Measurement — size, weight, length comparisons
  • Sorting and classifying by attribute
  • Introduction to addition through objects
🌿

World Around Us

  • Living vs. non-living things
  • Plants — parts, growth, what they need
  • Animals — habitats, food, movement
  • Weather — sun, rain, wind, seasons
  • The five senses as scientific tools
  • My body — parts, health, hygiene
  • Community helpers and their roles
🎨

Arts & Imagination

  • Drawing — self-portraits, nature, free expression
  • Colour mixing — primary and secondary colours
  • Music — rhythm, pitch, singing, simple instruments
  • Drama — role play, puppetry, storytelling
  • Construction play — building with blocks
  • Dance & movement to music
💛

Social-Emotional Learning

  • Naming and expressing emotions
  • Self-regulation — calming strategies
  • Empathy — understanding others' feelings
  • Friendship — sharing, taking turns, kindness
  • Problem-solving — conflict resolution basics
  • Resilience — trying again after difficulty
🏃

Movement & Physical Development

  • Gross motor — running, jumping, balancing
  • Fine motor — cutting, threading, clay, drawing
  • Body awareness and spatial understanding
  • Cooperative movement games
  • Healthy habits — sleep, food, hygiene
  • Outdoor exploration and nature play
1

The Sound Hunt — Phonics Introduction

LanguageAges 3–5Week 1
Learning Objectives
  • Identify initial sounds of familiar words
  • Match spoken sounds to letter symbols (s,a,t,p,i,n)
  • Develop phonemic awareness through rhyme and alliteration
Lesson Flow (45 min)
  • Warm-up (5 min): Clap-and-say syllable game with children's names
  • Story Time (10 min): Read a picture book — emphasise initial sounds aloud
  • Sound Hunt (15 min): Walk the room finding objects starting with "s" and "t"
  • Letter Craft (10 min): Trace and decorate letter cards with matching drawings
  • Reflection (5 min): Share one word that starts with today's sound
Materials

Letter cards, small objects from classroom, crayons, picture book

2

Counting With Creatures — Numbers 1–10

MathematicsAges 3–6Week 1
Learning Objectives
  • Count objects 1–10 with one-to-one correspondence
  • Recognize written numerals 1–10
  • Understand "more" and "less" through comparison
Lesson Flow (40 min)
  • Opening Song (5 min): "Five Little Ducks" — count down together
  • Counting Mats (15 min): Place animal counters on numbered mats 1–10
  • Story Problem (10 min): "4 butterflies. 2 more come. How many?" (use objects)
  • Number Writing (10 min): Trace and write numerals 1–5 in sand trays
Materials

Counting mats, animal counters or beans, sand trays, number cards

3

Seeds & Sprouts — How Plants Grow

ScienceAges 4–6
Learning Objectives
  • Name basic parts of a plant (root, stem, leaf, flower)
  • Understand what plants need to grow (water, sun, soil)
  • Make and record a simple scientific observation
Lesson Flow (50 min)
  • Story (10 min): "Jack and the Beanstalk" — how did the bean grow?
  • Planting (20 min): Each child plants a bean seed in a clear cup
  • Plant Parts Drawing (15 min): Draw and label a plant with teacher support
  • Science Journal (5 min): Draw what was planted — predict what it looks like in 1 week
Materials

Bean seeds, clear cups, soil, water, drawing paper, pencils/crayons

4

Feelings are Okay — Emotional Vocabulary

SELAges 3–6
Learning Objectives
  • Name at least 6 core emotions
  • Connect emotions to facial expressions and body sensations
  • Practice one calming strategy independently
Lesson Flow (40 min)
  • Mirror Play (5 min): Make faces — happy? Sad? Surprised?
  • Feelings Cards (10 min): Match emotion cards to story scenarios
  • Story (10 min): "The Colour Monster" — why is the monster confused?
  • Belly Breathing (10 min): Breathe in for 4, out for 4
  • Feelings Jar Craft (5 min): Colour your own feelings jar
Materials

Emotion face cards, "The Colour Monster", small mirrors, feelings jar printout

Colour Mixing Magic — Art & Science

ArtsScience
Learning Objectives
  • Name the three primary colours
  • Predict and observe what happens when two colours mix
  • Express creativity through free painting
Lesson Flow (45 min)
  • Discussion (5 min): What colours do you know? What's your favourite?
  • Discovery Experiment (20 min): Droppers and food colouring — mix red+yellow, blue+yellow, red+blue. Record on prediction sheet.
  • Free Painting (20 min): Large paper, full palette — create anything you imagine
Materials

Watercolour paints or food colouring, paper cups, droppers, large white paper

Mystery Bag Challenge

Place 5 objects in a bag. Without looking, feel each and describe: shape, texture, weight. Then guess what it is. Develops sensory vocabulary and inference.

The Ecosystem Web — How Everything is Connected

ScienceAges 7–9
Learning Objectives
  • Define producer, consumer, and decomposer
  • Trace a food chain through 4+ links
  • Explain what happens if one link is removed
Lesson Flow (60 min)
  • Hook (5 min): "What would happen if all bees disappeared?" Discussion.
  • Food Chain Yarn Web (20 min): Each student holds a "species" card. Use yarn to show connections. Then one "species" sits down — observe the cascade.
  • Diagram (15 min): Draw and label a food web with at least 6 organisms
  • Research (15 min): Find one endangered species and explain its role in an ecosystem
  • Discussion (5 min): Why does biodiversity matter?
2

Multiplication as Arrays — Visual Maths

MathematicsAges 7–8
Learning Objectives
  • Represent multiplication with arrays
  • Understand the commutative property (3×4 = 4×3)
  • Apply multiplication to solve real-world problems
Lesson Flow (55 min)
  • Open Question (5 min): "I have 4 rows of chairs with 6 in each row. How many without counting one by one?"
  • Array Building (20 min): Use counters to build arrays. Record as multiplication sentences.
  • Grid Paper (15 min): Draw arrays for 3×7, 5×4, 6×8. Calculate area.
  • Word Problems (15 min): Solve 5 real-world multiplication stories
3

Writing to Persuade — Your First Opinion Essay

Language ArtsAges 8–10
Learning Objectives
  • Understand the structure of a persuasive essay
  • Use at least 3 pieces of evidence to support an argument
  • Write a paragraph with a clear topic sentence
Lesson Flow (60 min)
  • Mentor Text (10 min): Read a model persuasive letter. Identify the claim and evidence.
  • Planning (15 min): Choose a topic. Fill in argument planner.
  • Draft (25 min): Write 3-paragraph essay using the planner
  • Peer Review (10 min): Exchange with a partner. Find: claim, evidence, one improvement.
4

Ancient Egypt — Civilisation & Innovation

Social StudiesAges 8–10
Learning Objectives
  • Explain why the Nile River was central to Egyptian civilisation
  • Describe 3 major achievements of ancient Egypt
  • Connect ancient innovations to modern equivalents
Lesson Flow (60 min)
  • Map Work (10 min): Locate Egypt, Nile, Sahara. Why did civilisation grow along the river?
  • Innovation Gallery (20 min): Rotate through 5 stations: pyramids, hieroglyphics, calendar, medicine, agriculture
  • Hieroglyphics Activity (15 min): Write their name in the ancient alphabet
  • Discussion (15 min): Which Egyptian invention would you most want to bring to life today, and why?

Scratch Storytelling — First Code Project

The Double Helix — DNA, Genes & Inheritance

BiologyAges 12–14
Learning Objectives
  • Describe the structure of DNA and role of genes
  • Explain dominant and recessive inheritance using Punnett squares
  • Discuss ethical implications of genetic technology
Lesson Flow (75 min)
  • Hook (5 min): "If you could know your future health risks, would you want to? Why?"
  • DNA Model (20 min): Build DNA using paper strips — base pairs, double helix, nucleotides
  • Punnett Squares (20 min): Eye colour, earlobe attachment — predict inheritance ratios
  • Case Study (20 min): CRISPR gene editing — benefits and risks
  • Ethics Debate (10 min): "Should parents choose their child's traits?"
2

Solving Linear Equations — Algebra Foundations

MathematicsAges 11–13
Learning Objectives
  • Solve one and two-step linear equations
  • Use inverse operations to isolate a variable
  • Apply algebra to model real-world problems
Lesson Flow (60 min)
  • Balance Model (10 min): Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other
  • Worked Examples (15 min): 3x + 7 = 22, then 2(x-3) = 10 step-by-step
  • Independent Practice (20 min): 10 equations of increasing difficulty
  • Application (15 min): "A taxi costs $3 plus $2/km. Total was $17. How far?" Write and solve the equation.
3

The Causes of World War One — Historical Analysis

HistoryAges 12–14
Learning Objectives
  • Identify the four MAIN causes (Militarism, Alliance, Imperialism, Nationalism)
  • Analyse primary source documents from 1914
  • Construct a causal argument using evidence
Lesson Flow (75 min)
  • Timeline (10 min): Key events June–August 1914 — assassination to declaration
  • Source Analysis (25 min): 3 primary sources: newspaper excerpts, political cartoons, telegrams
  • MAIN Jigsaw (20 min): Groups become experts on one cause, teach the class
  • Essay Planning (20 min): "Was the assassination of Franz Ferdinand the real cause of WWI?"

Supply, Demand & Market Equilibrium

EconomicsAges 14–16
Learning Objectives
  • Draw and interpret supply and demand diagrams
  • Explain how price signals allocate resources
  • Analyse what happens when supply or demand shifts
Lesson Flow (75 min)
  • Simulation (15 min): Classroom market — students are buyers and sellers of "apples" (cards). Observe price discovery in action.
  • Theory (20 min): Draw S&D diagram. Label axes, curves, equilibrium. Shift curves and explain.
  • News Application (20 min): Apply to 3 real current events (oil prices, housing, food shortage)
  • Case Study Essay (20 min): "Why did masks cost 10x more in March 2020?" — draw diagram, write explanation

The Cold War — Ideology, Proxy Wars & Nuclear Fear

HistoryAges 14–16
Learning Objectives
  • Explain how ideological differences led to the Cold War
  • Analyse the Cuban Missile Crisis as a near-miss nuclear event
  • Evaluate long-term impact of Cold War proxy conflicts
Lesson Flow (80 min)

Calculus — Derivatives and the Rate of Change

MathematicsAges 16–18
Learning Objectives
  • Understand the derivative as instantaneous rate of change
  • Apply differentiation rules: power, product, chain
  • Use derivatives to find maxima, minima, and optimise real problems
Lesson Flow (90 min)
  • Intuition (15 min): The speedometer is a derivative. If distance is a function, speed is its derivative.
  • Rules Practice (25 min): Differentiate 12 functions using power, product, and chain rules
  • Optimisation (30 min): "A farmer has 200m of fence. What dimensions give maximum area?" — solve with derivatives.
  • Applications (20 min): Marginal cost in economics, velocity in physics, population in biology

Rawls & Justice — What is a Fair Society?

PhilosophyAges 16–18
Learning Objectives
  • Explain Rawls' "veil of ignorance" thought experiment
  • Compare Rawlsian justice with libertarian and utilitarian alternatives
  • Apply the framework to design a just educational system
Lesson Flow (80 min)
  • Thought Experiment (15 min): "You're about to be born. You don't know if you'll be rich or poor, healthy or disabled, or which country. What society would you design?"
  • Rawls' Two Principles (20 min): Equal liberty principle and the difference principle
  • Comparison (20 min): How would Nozick (libertarian) and Bentham (utilitarian) disagree with Rawls?
  • Application (25 min): Design a "just" education system from behind the veil. Present and defend it.
